# 第7章 Blood Moon Infiltration

Five days later, they moved at dusk.
Ethan had forged documents to enter the palace under the guise of Council suppliers. Ella wore a hooded cloak to hide her face.
The palace was vast, carved from black stone. Torches lined the walls. They passed through the outer halls without incident—the guards were focused on the Blood Moon preparations in the central courtyard.
But as they reached the lower chambers, a figure blocked the passage.
Lydia Vance. Dressed in ceremonial robes. A cruel smile on her lips.
“I knew you’d come, cousin. You’re as predictable as your mother.”
Ella tensed. “I’m not your cousin.”
“She’s a traitor.” Lydia gestured, and a dozen armed guards emerged from side corridors. “Seize them.”
Ethan shifted—his body grew, claws extended. He tore through three guards before the others could react.
Ella used her vision to dodge a strike and grabbed a fallen guard’s dagger.
But Lydia was faster. She threw a blast of dark energy that sent Ella crashing into the wall.
“You think you can challenge the Council?” Lydia sneered. “You’re a half-breed whose only trick is seeing a few seconds ahead.”
Ella struggled to her feet. Her arm burned. The silver mark was glowing.
Then a voice echoed in her mind: *Daughter. Draw the moon’s power. Strike her with light.*
Ella raised her hand. She concentrated on the moon—the Blood Moon rising outside. A beam of silver light lanced from her palm, hitting Lydia’s chest.
Lydia stumbled back, robes smoking. “What—?”
Ethan finished off the last guard and grabbed Ella. “We don’t have much time. The ritual is starting.”
They ran down the stairs. The altar chamber was ahead.
Inside, the High Priest stood over a stone altar where Elena lay chained, pale and weak.
Marcus Vance stood at the head, a dark blade in his hand.
“Welcome, niece. You’re just in time for the final act.”
Ella charged.
But Marcus raised his hand, and a wave of force threw her back. She landed hard, her vision flickering.
Ethan lunged at Marcus, but the King’s guard intercepted—Jason, Ethan’s own second-in-command, holding a silver blade.
“I’m sorry, boss,” Jason said, face pained. “They have my family.”
Ethan gaped. “Jason?”
But Jason didn’t attack. He turned and stabbed the guard beside him.
“I’m not with them.” He threw Ethan a sword. “Go. I’ll hold the door.”
Ethan caught the sword and turned on Marcus.
The fight was brutal. Marcus was centuries old, fueled by stolen power. Ethan was younger, but his Lycan blood was pure.
Ella rose and ran to the altar. She pushed the chains, but they were enchanted.
“Mother.”
The name tore out of her—ten years of silence behind it.
Elena opened her eyes. Silver, like Ella’s. Thinner than any photograph. Hair dull against the stone.
“You came.” Elena’s voice broke on the second word. “My baby. You came.”
Ella gripped the bars until her knuckles whitened. “I’m here. I’m right here.”
“I watched you grow from far away. In flashes. In pain.” Elena pressed her fingers through the gap, searching for Ella’s skin. When they touched, both of them shuddered. “You’re so warm. I forgot what warm felt like.”
Ella leaned her forehead to the cold iron, as close as she could get. Tears ran hot down her cheeks. She didn’t try to stop them.
“I thought you were dead,” she said. “I thought I was alone.”
“Never alone.” Elena’s thumb traced her jaw, clumsy with weakness. “I sent what I could. The roses. The voice. Did you hear me?”
“I heard you.”
For a moment, the chamber faded—the chanting, Marcus, the Blood Moon overhead. There was only her mother’s hand on her face and the sound of two people breathing the same air again.
Then Elena’s eyes flicked toward the dais. Fear returned, sharp as a blade.
“You must flee. He is too strong.”
“No. I’m not leaving you.”
Elena’s hand tightened on Ella’s cheek. “The bond—you started it. You need to finish it. Only the full bond can give you the power to stop him.”
“With Ethan?”
“Yes. Now. While there is time.”
Ella looked back at Ethan fighting Marcus. He was bleeding. His arm was cut deep.
She reached through the bond. *Ethan. I’m ready. Do it now.*
He didn’t answer, but she felt his awareness. He threw a punch to create distance, then turned.
Marcus took the opening—a dagger of dark energy slammed into Ethan’s chest.
He dropped.
“Ethan!” Ella’s scream tore through the chamber.
