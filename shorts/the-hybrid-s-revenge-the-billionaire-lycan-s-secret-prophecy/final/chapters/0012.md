# 第12章 Marked Queen

Moonrise painted the courtyard silver.

Every pack that had signed the charter sent witnesses. Hybrids stood beside purebloods without segregation for the first time in living memory. Elena sat in the front row, wrapped in white fur, Jason and Bjorn flanking her like honor guard.

Ella wore a gown the color of ash with silver thread tracing the mark on her arm. Ethan wore black, his tie threaded with matching silver, jaw set against the stares that still cut.

The moon priest raised his hands. “Under the witness of the Blood Moon—”

A murmur ran through the crowd. Ella lifted her chin and let it pass. She had stood in worse rooms. She had survived worse nights.

“Do you, Ella Vance, take Ethan Blackwood as your true bonded, for life and beyond?”

The courtyard held its breath. Ella thought of the elevator. The cabin. The altar. Every moment she had almost walked away—and every moment she had stayed.

“I do.”

“Do you, Ethan Blackwood, take Ella Vance as your true bonded, for life and beyond?”

Ethan didn’t hesitate. His eyes never left hers. “I do.”

They cut their palms. Blood met blood.

Pain flared—then warmth. The scars from the altar, from the desperate night on the stone, flared bright and settled into a single pulsing line that ran wrist to wrist when their hands clasped.

The bond locked. What the altar had forged in blood, the moon now sealed by choice—not a contract, not a prophecy, but witnessed by every pack in the realm.

Ella felt it settle through her like a breath she had been holding for months.

The crowd howled.

She didn’t move at first. Neither did Ethan. They stood with their hands joined, forehead almost touching, letting the noise wash over them until it felt real.

Then she kissed him.

Ethan’s arms came around her, solid and real. She melted into it and let herself stay there a long moment before the world asked anything else of her.

When they parted, a strange flutter moved through her core—small, new, impossible to mistake.

Ethan felt it through the bond and went still. “Ella?”

She pressed his hand to her stomach, laughing once in disbelief. “Later.” She glanced at the crowd, at her mother in the front row. “Let them stare first.”

Elena stood, slowly, and the courtyard quieted. She didn’t speak long.

“She survived what I could not finish,” Elena said. “Watch her well.”

That was all. It was enough.

---

Later, on the balcony where Marcus once addressed loyalists, Ella leaned into Ethan’s shoulder. The city below was still scarred—broken gates, smoke stains, candles in windows—but alive.

“Are you afraid?” he asked.

“Of them?” She nodded toward the streets. “Every day. Of this?” She covered his hand on her belly. “No.”

Ethan was quiet a moment. “Your revenge is done.”

Ella watched the moon climb. “Then the rest is ours.”

Somewhere in the palace, Elena slept without chains for the first time in ten years. Somewhere in the city, a hybrid girl looked at her reflection and did not hide her eyes.

Ella laced her fingers through Ethan’s. The bond thrummed steady between them—not a chain, not a curse, but a choice made in public and kept in private.

Below them, the city lights flickered on one by one. Somewhere a hybrid child howled in celebration. Somewhere a pureblood officer threw his badge into the river. Change was never clean. It was only beginning.

Elena joined them on the balcony without announcement, wrapped in her white fur, and stood on Ella’s other side. No chains. No guards. Just a mother watching her daughter hold a kingdom and a mate at once.

“Ready?” he asked.

She smiled. “Not even close. But I’m not running.”

THE END
