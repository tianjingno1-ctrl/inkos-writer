# 第9章 The Crown Trial

Marcus was dead, but the palace did not go quiet.

Hybrid prisoners broke their chains in the lower cells. Pureblood guards split—some fled, some knelt, some reached for weapons they no longer believed in. Ella stood in the throne room with Elena leaning against her shoulder and Ethan barking orders through Jason’s comm link.

A messenger burst in, breathless. “The northern packs won’t accept a hybrid on the throne. Bjorn of the Frost Pack is marching south with two thousand wolves. Three days, maybe less.”

Ethan swore. “We don’t have three days.”

Elena lifted her head. Weak, but clear. “Then use the old law. Any alpha who rejects the heir may challenge for the crown—single combat, wolf against wolf. Win, and the packs bound by blood must yield.”

Ella looked at her mother, then at the empty throne. Marcus’s body had already been dragged away. The crown itself sat on a side table like an accusation.

“Broadcast it,” she said. “I’ll fight Bjorn at dawn.”

Ethan caught her wrist. “You’re bleeding from three fights already.”

“So is he, if he’s marching all night.” She pulled free, gently. “If I don’t prove myself now, we’ll spend the next year at war. I won’t do that to them.”

The word went out before midnight.

---

Ella didn’t sleep.

Ethan found her on the battlements after midnight, arms wrapped around herself, silver mark hidden under a borrowed coat. Below, campfires dotted the road north—Bjorn’s vanguard, close enough to smell smoke.

“You should rest,” he said.

“I keep seeing his teeth at my throat.” She didn’t look at him. “What if the Dawn Wolf isn’t enough?”

“It was enough at the altar.”

“That was rage. This is a courtroom with teeth.” She finally turned. “If I lose, they don’t just take the crown. They take you. They take my mother. They take every hybrid who believed me for five minutes.”

Ethan stepped close and brushed a streak of dried blood from her cheek. “Then don’t lose.”

“Helpful.”

“I’m serious.” His voice dropped. “Show them what you showed me when you pulled me back from the cable. Not the prophecy. You.”

She held his gaze until her pulse steadied.

Dawn came gray and cold.

---

Bjorn arrived at first light—a scarred alpha who stood a head taller than Ethan in human form and twice as wide in wolf. Hundreds filled the courtyard: northern warriors, freed hybrids, palace staff who didn’t know yet which side would feed them.

“So you’re the half-blood queen,” Bjorn called up. “You don’t look like much.”

Ella stripped off her torn jacket. “Looks lie.”

They shifted.

Bjorn became a wall of brown fur and muscle. Ella’s form answered in silver and white, light streaking along her spine—the Dawn Wolf, raw and new and shaking with fatigue. A murmur rolled through the crowd.

They collided.

Bjorn was strong in the way of old alphas who had never lost territory. Every charge came heavy, meant to break ribs. Ella didn’t meet force with force. Her sight opened a heartbeat ahead—paw low, jaw high, feint left—and she moved where he would not be.

Minutes stretched. Her lungs burned. Bjorn slowed, surprised that she was still standing.

He lunged for her throat.

She saw it before he committed, dropped under the arc, and pinned him with her forepaw at his neck. Her muzzle pressed against his fur.

“Yield.”

For a long second, nothing moved.

Then Bjorn went still beneath her. His wolf lowered its head to the stones.

“I yield.”

The courtyard erupted.

Ella shifted back, naked and shaking, and accepted a cloak from Jason without looking away from Bjorn. She offered the alpha her hand. He took it, human again, blood on his lip.

“Rise, Alpha,” she said. “We fight the loyalists together now.”

Bjorn studied her face, then nodded once. “You have the Frost Pack.”

Elena was crying quietly in the front row. Ethan stood beside her, pride and fear warring in his eyes.

Ella crossed to her mother and knelt. “Did I do enough?”

“For today.” Elena cupped her face, thumbs brushing the tears Ella hadn’t noticed. “You were always enough. I just couldn’t tell you from where they kept me.”

Ella leaned into her mother’s hands and let herself breathe. The courtyard noise faded to a hum.

After a moment, Elena’s voice turned urgent again. “Tomorrow they’ll test you again. Before they do—take what your father left. Under the throne. The vault only opens for his bloodline, and you’re the last one standing.”

Ella looked at Ethan. “Now?”

“Now,” Elena said. “Before the next horn sounds.”

They ran back inside. Guards lifted the stone slab beneath the carpet. A spiral stair swallowed torchlight.

Ella took the first step down.

Two hundred feet below, something pulsed in the dark—and above them, distant as thunder, horns blared at the palace wall.
