# 第8章 The Bond

Ella threw herself at Marcus, a wave of silver fire erupting from her hands. He blocked, surprised by her strength. But she wasn’t aiming for him—she grabbed Ethan and pulled him behind the altar.
Ethan’s breath was shallow. Blood pooled under his chest.
“You’re going to be fine,” she said, tears streaming. “You have to be fine.”
He smiled weakly. “Finish it. The bond—our blood.”
Ella looked at her mother on the altar—Elena watching them, lips moving in a prayer Ella couldn’t hear. Then back at Ethan. The man who had waited. Who had not lied about the cost.
She cut her palm again. The pain was nothing.
“I promise,” she said. “I trust you.”
She pressed her blood to his. Their scars from the cabin flared, seeking each other.
“I—” The word stuck. Love was too large and too soon and exactly true.
She kissed him instead.
For a heartbeat, nothing happened. Then the mark flared. Power rushed through her—a door swinging open all the way.
She felt his life force draining, but she poured her own into him—her vision energy, her hybrid blood. The wound began to close.
Ethan’s eyes snapped open. The iris caught fire—gold and silver. He drew a shaky breath, human still, and caught her wrist.
“I’m here,” he said. Rough. Certain.
“I know.” She felt him through the bond—not just power, but presence. Steady.
Then he roared, shifting fully into a massive black wolf.
Marcus stepped back. “Impossible. The bond—it holds that much power?”
Ella stood. Silver flame wreathed her body. The bond hummed in her chest, Ethan’s heartbeat layered over hers. Power enough to save him. The realm would have to wait for the rest.
“It’s over, Uncle.”
Jason had cleared the remaining guards and was helping Elena off the altar.
Marcus laughed. “You think a bond makes you strong? Let me show you true power.”
He raised his hands. The altar chamber shook. The Blood Moon above bled into a black eclipse.
Elena screamed. “He’s activating the curse now—he’ll drain all hybrids!”
Ella looked at Ethan. He was huge, but he was hurting.
“Together,” she said.
He nodded.
They attacked as one. Ella’s silver flame and Ethan’s wolf strength. Marcus deflected them, but they kept pressing.
Ella saw his moves a second ahead. She called out directions to Ethan.
They forced Marcus back to the edge of the altar platform.
Then Marcus made a mistake. He overextended his shield.
Ella poured all her energy into a single spear of light. It pierced him through the chest.
Marcus stared, disbelieving. “You—you can’t—I am eternal—”
He crumpled.
The black eclipse shattered. The Blood Moon returned to normal.
And in the distance, the curse’s chains broke.
The chamber went still.

Ella fell to her knees. Elena caught her—real arms, thin but strong, pulling her close for the first time since Ella was a child. She smelled of stone and old blood and something underneath that was still, impossibly, her mother.
“I’ve got you,” Elena whispered into her hair. “I’ve got you now.”
Ella sobbed into her shoulder. All the years she had smiled in offices and counted to five and slept with the lights on poured out of her at once. She didn’t try to be brave. She clung to her mother and let herself shake.
“You did it, daughter.”
“You’re alive.” Ella pulled back enough to see her face. Traced the silver at her temples with trembling fingers. “You’re really alive.”
“For now.” Elena smiled through tears of her own. “For now is enough.”
Ethan hung back by the shattered altar, giving them space. Blood dried on his shirt. When Ella looked at him, he nodded once—not rushing her.
She wiped her face with the back of her hand. Her mother’s hand stayed in hers.
“We do it together,” Ella said. “All three of us.”
Elena squeezed her fingers. “Always.”
Ethan crossed the room then, slowly, and set his hand on Ella’s shoulder. She leaned into the touch without letting go of her mother.
Only when Elena’s breathing steadied did Jason speak from the doorway. “Boss. We need to move.”
Outside the chamber, horns sounded—loyalists rallying to retake the palace before dawn.
