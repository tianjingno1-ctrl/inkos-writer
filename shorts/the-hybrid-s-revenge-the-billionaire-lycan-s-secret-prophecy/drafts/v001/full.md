# The Hybrid’s Revenge: The Billionaire Lycan’s Secret Prophecy

## 开篇钩子

Ella’s vision bled red. The elevator cables snapped. She lunged without thinking—her fingers locked onto the man’s sleeve and yanked him sideways. The broken cable slammed into the floor where he’d stood a heartbeat ago.
Ethan Blackwood stared down at her. His voice came low, rough. “The prophecy says when the hybrid appears, her eyes will flash silver.” He caught her chin, tilting her face to the light. “Yours just did.”
A sting burned through her arm. And beneath his tailored suit, his palm split open—the mark of the royal bond waking to its match.
She tore away, stumbling down the stairs. She didn’t see the white rose petal that fell from his hand to the tile.

## 第1章 Traces of Prophecy

Ella Vance kept her head down as the three pureblood women walked past her desk.
“Morning, half-breed. Try not to breathe our air.” Cassie’s coffee cup tipped deliberately—lukewarm liquid splashed across Ella’s keyboard and dripped onto her skirt. The other two laughed.
Ella pressed her lips together. Her nails dug into her palm. Count to ten. Don’t shift. Don’t let them see anything.
She blotted the spill without a word. Behind her, someone whispered “pathetic” and the word stung worse than the coffee.
At five thirty, she packed her bag and walked to the elevator. The day had been long. Another meeting where Lydia Vance—the pureblood executive who happened to share her last name but no blood—had openly suggested she was “better suited for janitorial work.”
Ella had smiled.
She always smiled.
The elevator doors slid open. She stepped inside, and a man in a charcoal suit followed. Ethan Blackwood. CEO. He stood taller than she remembered, his presence making the mirrored box feel smaller.
The car began to rise.
Then Ella’s head exploded with light.
Steel screaming. Sparks showering. Cables whipping like vipers. She saw the floor number blink—4—and the ceiling crack open. The vision poured through her faster than thought.
She moved. Her hand shot out, fisted in his sleeve, and she wrenched him backward so hard he stumbled against the wall.
The cable burst through the floor panel exactly where he’d been standing. It curled, smoking, the severed end a claw of twisted metal.
Silence.
Then his voice, calm and dark. “You just saved my life.”
Ella’s legs shook. She released his sleeve. “I… I saw something. I don’t know why I grabbed you.”
He didn’t step away. His eyes locked onto hers—deep gold-brown, like sunlight through amber. “Your eyes,” he said, “they flashed silver. The old texts call it the eye of the prophecy. A hybrid’s mark.”
Her heart stopped. She opened her mouth to deny it, but a burning sensation crawled under her right arm. She didn’t need to look. She knew what was there.
He leaned in. “I’ve been waiting for you, Ella.”
She shoved past him and bolted for the stairwell. Her boots slammed against concrete as she ran. She didn’t stop until she was home in her cramped apartment, door locked.
Ella yanked off her blazer, pushed up her sleeve. The skin on her forearm shimmered—a faint silver pattern snaking like veins. She touched it. Pain flared.
She stumbled to the bathroom mirror. Stared at her reflection.
Nothing. Just her usual brown eyes.
But then a flicker. Irises bleeding silver.
She gasped and jerked back, knocking a glass off the counter. It shattered. When she looked again, the silver was gone.
Her phone buzzed. A text from an unknown number: *You are not safe. But I can protect you. – E.B.*
She threw the phone onto the couch and wrapped her arms around herself.
The silver didn’t come back that night. But the burn kept its rhythm, pulsing like a second heartbeat.

## 第2章 The Promotion Trap

Ella arrived at work the next morning to find the office buzzing. A group of assistants had gathered near the coffee station, and heads turned when she walked in.
Cassie blocked her. “Looks like the half-breed lucked her way into the CEO’s special little circle.” She sneered. “Private assistant to Ethan Blackwood.”
Ella’s stomach dropped.
The email had come in at six a.m. By the time she reached her desk, there was a printed letter waiting, with Ethan’s signature at the bottom.
She didn’t have time to process before Lydia Vance appeared, clicking across the marble floor. “This is a mistake,” Lydia said, loud enough for everyone to hear. “She doesn’t have the qualifications. She’s a temp.”
Ella looked at Lydia. Pureblood. Her father’s cousin. The woman who’d made sure Ella’s first year at Redwood was a slow, grinding humiliation. Lydia had the power to fire her any time, but she never did. She liked the sport.
“The decision is made,” Ethan’s voice cut through from behind him. He stood at the elevator, holding the door. “Ella, with me.”
She grabbed her bag and walked toward him. The whole floor watched.
In the private elevator, she said, “I didn’t ask for this.”
“You didn’t need to.” He pressed a button for the top floor. “The building is full of eyes that want you dead. My office is safer than that communal pit.”
“And if I don’t want your protection?”
“Then you won’t survive the week.”
The doors opened onto a wide, glass-walled office. Spreadsheets on screens. Models of construction projects. But her attention was on the file open on his desk—a photograph of a woman with silver-streaked hair. Her mother.
Ella’s throat closed.
Ethan set a cup of water in front of her. “You recognize her?”
She didn’t answer.
“She was my father’s advisor. Before the old king was killed.”
The mention of the king sent a chill through her. She wanted to ask more, but a flash of heat pulsed in her arm. The silver pattern snaked up her shoulder, hidden under her blouse.
She excused herself to the restroom.
In the stall, she lifted her sleeve. The symbol had grown. It looked almost like words now, but not any language she knew.
When she came out, Lydia was there. Washing her hands. Their eyes met in the mirror.
“You think he’s protecting you,” Lydia said, drying her fingers slowly. “He isn’t. You’re a weapon he found. Once the trigger’s pulled, he’ll use you until you break.”
Ella said nothing.
“I’ve already started the report to the Elder Council. By the end of this month, they’ll know exactly what you are.”
Lydia left.
Ella stayed in the bathroom until her hands stopped shaking.
That night, she returned to her apartment to find a single white rose on her doorstep. And a letter printed in elegant script: *Your mother used to ask me to give you these. I am still bound to her wish. —A friend.*
She turned the flower in her hands. A friend. Or a trap.
The silver wound on her arm throbbed in time with her heartbeat.

## 第3章 First Blood

The car park was dark.
Ella had stayed late, catching up on files that Lydia had “accidentally” dumped on her. By the time she reached the ground floor, the building was mostly empty.
Three men stepped out from behind a pillar.
She recognized them as pureblood enforcers from the Vance family. Their eyes glinted in the fluorescent light.
“We just need to confirm something,” the leader said, rolling his shoulders. “Standard sniff test. If you’re clean, we’ll let you walk.”
Ella’s chest tightened. A sniff test meant wolf senses—they’d be able to smell the anomaly in her blood. The hybrid taint.
“I work here,” she said, keeping her voice flat. “You don’t have the right.”
“We have every right.” He stepped closer. “Orders from the Council. Any suspected hybrid on Redwood property gets checked.”
She backed up. Her heels hit concrete.
And then the world split.
A vision swept through her—the enforcer’s fist, moving toward her face. She saw the exact angle, the speed, the arc. She saw herself crumple.
No. She didn’t want to.
A heat erupted in her arm, and her muscles loosened. The vision was already fading, but she held the shape of it. When the fist came, she was ready. She ducked.
The enforcer missed.
He blinked. “What the—”
“Enough.”
Ethan stepped out of the shadows behind the men. He didn’t raise his voice, but the air pressed down like a weight.
“She’s under my protection,” he said. “Leave now or suffer the consequences.”
The enforcer hesitated. Then he spat at Ella’s feet and turned. The others followed.
Ella let out a breath.
Ethan took her arm and led her to his car. “They could have killed you.”
“They didn’t.”
“Because I showed up. Luck isn’t a strategy.”
In the car, she stared out the window. “Why do you care if I live or die?”
He drove in silence for a block before answering. “Because without you, I can’t bring down the Council. And they murdered my family. That’s the truth you asked for.”
She said nothing.
At her apartment, she got out, but he held up a hand. “There’s a car watching this street. Two enforcers. You’re not sleeping here tonight.”
She looked. A black sedan idled at the corner.
“Then where?”
Ethan pulled a card from his visor. An address in the suburbs. “Go pack a bag. Five minutes. I’ll wait.”
She went inside. Her hands shook as she threw clothes into a duffel. On the bed, the white rose lay where she’d left it. She tucked it into the bag.
In the bathroom, the silver pattern on her arm was glowing. Faint. Fading.
She covered it with a sleeve.
When she came out, Ethan was standing by the open door. The sedan had moved closer.
“Time to go.”
She got into his car, and they pulled away into the night.

## 第4章 The Safe House

The house sat at the end of a gravel road, surrounded by trees. It looked ordinary—two stories, peeling paint, a porch with one loose step. But the wards Ella felt when she stepped inside told another story. Protection symbols etched into the doorframe. Silver laced into the walls.
“My grandmother’s place,” Ethan said, flicking on a lamp. “Off the grid. No digital trail.”
Ella dropped her duffel on the floor. “How long do I have to stay here?”
“Until we decide the next move.”
She wanted to argue, but the exhaustion was a river pulling her under. She sat on the couch, and the cushions sank. “You said the Council killed your family. My family too, didn’t they? The same people.”
Ethan sat across from her. “Your father was the Lycan king. The Elder Council framed him for treason. Your uncle—Marcus Vance—took the throne. Your mother was the seer, kept alive to power their blood rites.”
Ella’s fingers tightened. “Alive?”
“Yes. She’s in the palace dungeons. I’ve seen her in my own visions.”
Ella’s throat burned. “You’re a seer?”
“No. But I have some of the old blood, from my mother’s line. Enough to sense certain things.” He leaned forward. “Your mother is alive, Ella. And the Council wants to use you as the final key to a blood curse that will enslave every hybrid in existence.”
The words hung in the air.
From her bag, she pulled out the white rose. It was still fresh, despite the day. “Someone left this for me at my apartment. It used to be my mother’s favorite.”
Ethan looked at the rose. His expression flickered. “That’s a bloom from the royal greenhouse.”
“Meaning?”
“Meaning there’s someone inside the palace who’s trying to reach you.”
Ella held the flower to her chest. Her mind churned. Trust no one. But she had no other threads.
That night, she lay on the fold-out bed, staring at the ceiling. She pushed at her gift, trying to see anything. Only darkness.
Then a whisper, like wind through a crack: *Don’t trust him either. Trust the blood.*
She sat up, cold sweat on her neck.
The room was empty.
But on the bedside table, a small silver ring had appeared. She didn’t remember it being there before.
She picked it up. Engraved on the inside: *For our daughter—Elena.*
Her mother’s ring.
Ella slid it onto her finger. It fit perfectly.

## 第5章 Training Teeth

The next morning, Ethan found Ella in the kitchen, staring at the ring.
“Where did you get that?”
“It was on my nightstand when I woke up.” She turned it over. “My mother’s.”
His eyes narrowed. “The safe house is warded. No one can get in without my knowing. So either the wards are weaker than I thought, or your mother’s magic is strong enough to bypass them.”
Ella held the ring tighter. “She’s reaching me.”
“That means she’s alive, and she’s still using her power. But every time she does, it drains her. We need to move fast.”
Ethan set a coffee in front of her and pulled out a chair. “First lesson: controlling the visions. You need to trigger them on command, or they’ll trigger you. At the worst moments.”
They went outside into the backyard. The trees formed a natural clearing.
“I’m going to attack you,” Ethan said.
“What?”
“You saw one punch coming yesterday. That was reflex. But you need to see more than one step ahead. I’ll come at you. You have to use the sight to counter me.”
He didn’t wait for her to agree. He lunged.
Ella barely had time to raise her hands. Her vision didn’t flicker. She took a blow to the shoulder, stumbling.
“Again.”
He swung. This time she saw the flash: his fist curving left. She ducked right. He adjusted, and she didn’t see that part, so his other hand caught her ribs.
She gasped, bent over.
“You only see the immediate threat,” Ethan said, not unkindly. “Your survival instinct guides it. You have to push deeper. Try to see my whole sequence.”
She straightened. Anger helped. Focus.
He came again.
This time the vision expanded: he would feint high, kick low. She sidestepped the kick and slammed her palm into his chest. He staggered back, eyes sharp.
“Better.”
They trained for an hour. Each success gave her a head rush. Each failure a bruise.
By the end, she could reliably see one full second ahead. Not much, but it was a start.
Back inside, she collapsed onto the couch. Her whole body ached. But the ring on her finger pulsed with heat, and she heard that whisper again: *Three days. Blood Moon. The ritual begins. Stop it before the altar drinks my soul.*
Ella jerked upright.
Ethan was watching her from the doorway. “What did you see?”
“She told me the Blood Moon is in three days. They’re going to use her as the sacrifice.”
He went still. “That lines up with the intelligence I’ve gathered.”
“Then we’re not waiting. We go in before that.”
Ethan shook his head. “You’re not ready. One second of foresight won’t save you against a full coven of pureblood assassins.”
“I don’t care. She’s my mother.”
He looked at her for a long moment. Then he nodded slowly. “We’ll need backup. And a clear plan.”
“I know one person who might help.” Ella held up the white rose. “But first, I need to know who sent this.”

## 第6章 The Diary

Ethan led her to a hidden room behind the fireplace. Bookshelves crammed with worn leather tomes. A desk covered in maps and letters. In the corner, a small steel safe.
He dialed the combination, pulled out a book wrapped in cloth. “This belonged to your mother. I had it recovered six years ago from the palace library after the purge.”
Ella took it. The cover was dark blue, embossed with silver lettering: *Elena Vance, Prophecies of Blood and Bond.*
She sat down and opened it.
The handwriting was delicate, looping. Dated twenty-three years ago. Her mother’s thoughts at seventeen.
She turned pages. Prophecies—some coded, some plain. Maps of old Lyan territories. And then, midway through, a section labeled “For my child.”
Ella’s breath caught.
*If you’re reading this, I may be gone. But know this: Marcus is not your uncle by blood alone. He was the one who betrayed your father. He struck the deal with the Elder Council for power. He chained me in the depths so he could feed on my seer energy to sustain the blood curse.*
*You are the only one who can break it. You have two bloodlines: my foresight and your father’s Lycan kingship. When these merge fully, you will become the Dawn Wolf—a being of silver flame that burns pureblood tyranny. But to merge them, you must be bound by true love’s mark. Not a contract. Not a convenience. Truth.*
Ella read the last line again. *True love’s mark.*
She looked up at Ethan. He was watching her, expression unreadable.
“My mother wrote that I need a true bond to unlock the full power.”
“I know.”
She waited.
He ran a hand through his hair. “I’ll admit, when I first found you, I thought we could fake it. Claim we were marked. Give you legal protection. But after last night, seeing what you can do…” He paused. “Faking it would kill us both. When you stand before the Blood Moon altar, the curse will test the bond. If it’s false, it will consume you.”
Ella set the diary down. “Then we have a problem.”
“Do we?”
She looked at him. He was close now. His voice lower. “I didn’t expect to care whether you lived. But I do.”
Ella’s heart pounded. “This isn’t the time—”
“No, it is exactly the time. Because the Blood Moon is three days away. And if we’re going to face it together, I need to know if you feel anything real, or if I’m just a tool for your revenge.”
She held his gaze. She thought about the way he’d thrown himself in front of her. The way his eyes didn’t look at her like a half-breed. The way her body hummed when he was near.
“I don’t know if it’s love,” she said. “But it’s not nothing.”
Ethan’s mouth lifted at the corner. “That’s enough to start.”
He took her hand. And in that touch, something passed between them—a current that made the silver on her arm glow bright.
Outside, the wind picked up, carrying the scent of iron.
The Blood Moon was rising.

## 第7章 Blood Moon Infiltration

They moved at night.
Ethan had a contact inside the palace—an old servant who owed his life to Elena. The servant left a gate unlocked near the old catacombs.
Ella wore black. Her arm burned under the sleeve, but she kept her breathing steady. Beside her, Ethan moved like a shadow.
They entered the catacombs. The air was damp, carrying the metallic tang of old blood. Torches flickered in iron sconces.
“The altar is in the central chamber,” Ethan whispered. “We bypass the guards, find your mother, and get out before the ceremony begins.”
Ella nodded. Her vision pulsed faintly—she could see three seconds ahead now, after two more hours of training that evening. It wasn’t perfect, but it was enough to spot the patrol pattern.
They slipped past two guards on the upper level. Then down a spiral staircase.
At the bottom, a wide corridor opened. At the end, iron bars.
Inside, a figure lay on a stone slab. Thin, pale hair splayed around her face. Her chest moved shallowly.
Ella’s legs carried her before she could think. “Mom.”
Elena opened her eyes—silver, just like Ella’s. There was recognition. And pain.
“You came.” A whisper, like glass breaking.
Ella reached through the bars, gripping her mother’s hand. “I’m getting you out.”
“No.” Elena squeezed back, weak but fierce. “The ritual begins at midnight. If I’m not on the altar when they call, they’ll know someone interfered. They’ll hunt you down.”
“Then we fight.”
“You’re not ready to face the full Council.”
“I don’t care.”
A sound echoed from the corridor above. Footsteps.
Ethan cursed. “They found the gate.”
Ella stood. Her vision rippled—she saw a squad of armed purebloods descending the stairs. Eight in the lead. More behind.
She looked at her mother. Elena pressed something into her palm: a small stone, warm as blood. “Take this. It holds a fragment of my power. Use it when you need the second awakening.”
Ella pocketed it.
“Go,” Elena said. “I will stall the ritual long enough for you to escape. Come back when the mark is true.”
“I’m not leaving you—”
“You must. Or we both die.” Elena’s eyes flared with sudden strength. “Live, daughter. Live and come back for me.”
Ethan grabbed Ella’s arm. The footsteps were louder.
She turned and ran.
They burst out of the catacombs into a moonlit courtyard. Shouts behind them. Arrows whistled past.
Ella’s vision showed her the trajectory—she ducked, pulled Ethan sideways. They reached the wall, scaled it.
In the forest beyond, they kept running until her lungs burned.
When they stopped, bent over, gasping, Ella pulled the warm stone from her pocket. A light pulsed inside it. Her mother’s voice, barely there: *Three days. Blood Moon. Return.*
Ella closed her fist.
She would be ready.

## 第8章 Bleeding for Him

The safe house felt colder now.
Ella sat on the floor, the hearth fire crackling. The stone from her mother lay in her open palm, dark and still. She’d been trying to draw power from it, but nothing happened.
Ethan was at the table, cleaning a dagger. His sleeves were rolled up, and she saw the silver scars on his forearms—a full Lycan’s marks from years of battle.
“We can’t wait three days,” she said.
He looked up. “We have no choice. The Blood Moon amplifies the ritual. We need the moon phase to open the seal on your mother’s cell.”
“We could break the seal without the moon. Use explosives.”
“That would kill her. The seal is tied to her life force. We have to wait for the natural thinning of the barrier.” He set the dagger down. “I don’t like it either.”
Silence.
Then he came closer, knelt in front of her. “There’s another problem. The bond. We need to mark each other before the altar test, or the magic will reject us.”
She met his eyes. “We don’t even know if it’s real enough.”
“We won’t know until we try. But I’m willing to find out if you are.”
Ella’s chest rose and fell. She thought of her mother’s diary: *True love’s mark.* Not a business arrangement. Something deeper.
“How does it work?”
“It requires blood exchange at the throat, where the wolf vein runs. And a spoken vow. The bond will only take if both hearts are in it equally.”
She swallowed. “And if one of us isn’t?”
“The pain will be immediate and severe. It could kill the one who forces it.”
Ella looked at his hands, the calluses from years of war. Then back at his face—hard lines, soft eyes.
“I don’t know if I love you,” she said. “But I know I trust you. That’s the first time I’ve felt that about anyone since my parents died.”
Ethan took her hand, turning it over. He pulled a small knife from his belt. “If you’re not ready, we don’t have to.”
“We do.” She steadied her breath. “Let’s try.”
He brought the knife to his own wrist. Cut a shallow line. Blood welled, rich and dark.
Then he cut her wrist—her Lycan half accepted the pain, muscles relaxed.
He pressed their wounds together.
A shock. It ran through her arm, up her neck, into her chest. Her blood felt like fire. She gritted her teeth.
Ethan’s jaw tightened. His eyes blazed gold.
Then the world tilted.
She saw fragments—his memories of a burning house, a woman screaming, a child’s hand reaching for a doorsill. Pain. Loss. Anger. An oath to never be powerless again.
He saw hers too: a shadowed crib, cruel laughter, a silver locket stolen. A girl who learned to smile so no one would see her bleeding.
It hurt. It also healed.
When she opened her eyes, Evan was holding her. His forehead pressed to hers.
“It’s real,” he said. Voice ragged. “It’s real.”
The mark on her arm blazed silver-white, then settled.
She was bound.
And her power surged—she saw the clock on the wall, heard the whisper of wind miles away, sensed the heartbeat of her mother underground.
She pushed to her feet.
“We go tonight,” she said. “I’m strong enough now.”
Ethan stood with her, blood still drying on his wrist. “Tonight.”
The fire popped.
Outside, the sky began to redden.

## 第9章 The Wolf’s Choice

They moved through the forest toward the palace. Halfway there, Ethan stopped.
A figure stepped from behind a tree. Jason. His second-in-command.
“Boss.” Jason’s voice strained. He held a gun, but it was pointed at the ground. “Lydia told me to lead a squad to intercept you. She has my family.”
Ethan’s expression hardened. “Where are they?”
“Held in the eastern wing. Three guards. If I don’t bring her in, they die.”
Ella watched the exchange. Her new bond let her feel Ethan’s pulse. He was trying to stay calm.
“You’ve been with me for six years, Jason. I’ve trusted you with every op.”
Jason’s jaw tightened. “She threatened my daughter.”
Ethan took a step forward. “Then we get your daughter out. Together.”
The gun in Jason’s hand wavered. “I gave her the location of the safe house. I’m the reason they found us at the catacombs.”
“I know.”
The words hung.
Ella saw Jason’s guilt in a flash—the shame was eating him. But she also saw his loyalty. He’d come here alone. He could have brought the whole squad.
“I’m tired of being afraid,” Jason said. “I want to stop.”
He lowered the gun to his side.
Then he raised it again—but turned and fired into the trees. A pureblood tracker crumpled from behind a log.
“Follow me,” Jason said. “I know a back path into the east wing. We free my family, then we hit the altar.”
Ethan nodded once. “Lead.”
They ran.
Twenty minutes later, they emerged into a narrow courtyard. A door. Jason’s code worked. They slipped into a servant corridor.
They found the cell where his wife and daughter were held. The guards were fast, but Ella’s sight let her predict their moves—she disarmed one with a joint lock, the other with a blow from Ethan. They didn’t kill. Noise would bring more.
Jason’s daughter squeezed his hand. “Daddy.”
He kissed her forehead. “Go with Ethan’s driver. He’ll take you safe.”
The girl ran.
Now they had an ally. They had a path.
And the moon was climbing.
Ella felt the pull toward the altar room. Her mother’s life force pulsed through the bond.
“We need to hurry. The ritual starts within the hour.”
They jogged through the lower passages, past empty guardrooms. The palace was hushed, like everyone else had gathered at the main courtyard.
They reached the door to the grand sanctuary.
Voices inside. Chanting.
Ella pushed the door open a crack.
The altar stood in the center. Her mother lay chained on it, white dress stained with old blood.
And at the altar’s altar, Marcus Vance stood, arms raised, silver fire wreathing his hands.
“Tonight,” he intoned, “the hybrid’s blood will seal the curse. And all half-bloods will bow or burn.”
Ella’s arm burned with answering heat.
It was time.

## 第10章 The Broadcast

Ella threw open the door.
The chanting stopped. Five hundred faces turned toward her. Councillors, nobles, guards. And on the raised dais, Marcus Vance lowered his hands.
“Guests,” he said, a smile spreading across his face. “The hybrid saves me the trouble of hunting her.”
Ella stepped forward. Ethan and Jason flanked her. “I’m not here to be your sacrifice. I’m here to end you.”
Marcus laughed. “With what army?”
She reached into her pocket and pulled out a small device—a portable jammer with a feed link. She pressed the button. The holographic projectors around the sanctuary flickered to life, broadcasting to every screen in the kingdom.
“This is Ella Vance,” she said, her voice echoing. “Daughter of King Aldric and the Seer Elena. My uncle Marcus murdered his own brother, imprisoned my mother, and has used her blood to sustain a curse that kills hybrids. Tonight, I’m going to prove it.”
Marcus’s face went cold. “Kill her.”
But the guards hesitated. The broadcast was live.
Ella didn’t wait. She used the moment of hesitation to call on her power. The bond with Ethan thrummed in her chest, and she drew from it. The silver flames on her arm erupted—not just on her skin, but outward. A blast of silver light shot across the hall, shattering the chains on her mother’s altar.
Elena sat up, gasping.
Ella ran to her, pulled her upright. “Can you walk?”
Elena nodded, though her legs shook. “You’re marked. You activated the bond.”
“Yes.”
Elena looked at Ethan and understood. “Then the dawn is coming.”
Marcus screamed an order, and guards rushed.
But the broadcast was spreading. Across the kingdom, hybrids saw a woman who looked like them fighting back. The image of Marcus chanting over a bound woman went out in high definition.
Then the first hybrid families began to arrive. Not as an army—but as witnesses. They blocked the palace exits, chanting for freedom.
Marcus turned purple with rage. He raised his hand and blasted silver lightning at his niece.
Ella threw up a shield of pure flame. The collision shook the hall.
“You can’t win,” Marcus snarled. “I have centuries of stolen power.”
“You have nothing but stolen time.”
She pushed back. The shield grew.
The bond with Ethan poured more strength into her. And from her mother—a steady pulse of prophecy, showing her Marcus’s next move before he made it.
He would feint left, then summon a blood whip.
She dropped the shield, sidestepped the whip, and countered with a spear of silver fire.
It struck his chest.
Marcus fell, screaming, as the fire consumed the stolen power stored in his blood.
The hall went silent.
The broadcast feed showed him writhing on the ground.
And then it was over.
Ella stood in the center of the sanctuary, breathing hard. The chains on her mother were gone. The curse was broken with the Council leader’s defeat.
But her mother was fading.
Elena sank to her knees.
“No.” Ella dropped beside her. “No, you’re free now. You have to stay.”
Elena smiled, touching Ella’s face. “The prophecy is complete. The hybrid has risen. My part is done.”
Her hand fell.
She was gone.

## 第11章 The Sacrifice

Ella didn’t expect the silence to feel so loud.
She held her mother’s body as the crowd in the sanctuary watched. The broadcast had cut off after Marcus fell, but the news had spread faster than emergency services.
Ethan knelt beside her, his hand on her shoulder.
“She held on long enough to see you win,” he said quietly.
Ella nodded, though the tears came anyway. She’d imagined this moment for years—freeing her mother, watching her heal. Not this.
But there was no time to grieve.
From the doorway, a voice cut through: “Ella Vance.”
She looked up. An Elder she didn’t recognize—an older woman with a scar down her cheek—stood in the doorway, flanked by two guards holding a white flag.
“The Elder Council is in emergency session. We’ve witnessed the broadcast. Marcus’s crimes are undeniable. But the governance of the Lycan nation must continue. We need a ruler.”
Ella’s jaw tightened. “I’m not interested in a throne.”
“You are the last remaining child of King Aldric. By law, the crown falls to you.”
She looked at her mother’s face, peaceful now. She thought of all the suffering the pureblood hierarchy had caused. Could she simply walk away?
Ethan said, “If she takes the throne, she’d have the power to dismantle the blood laws.”
The Elder nodded. “We are prepared to negotiate blood equality. The Council has seen the cost of tyranny.”
Ella wiped her face with the back of her hand. She stood, still holding her mother’s hand. “Then I’ll accept. But only under my terms.”
“Name them.”
“First: all pureblood privilege laws are abolished. Second: hybrids get full citizenship and representation in the Council. Third: my mother gets a state funeral with honors.”
The Elder paused, then inclined her head. “Agreed on all conditions. Formal coronation will take place in one week, after the funeral.”
Ella nodded.
She turned back to her mother’s body, and the tears returned. But this time, they came with a sense of purpose.
She would change this world.
That night, in the private chambers of the palace, she sat alone. Ethan entered, carrying a tray of tea. He didn’t say anything. He just sat beside her.
After a long moment, she spoke. “I don’t know if I can be queen.”
“You already are.” He took her hand. “You’re the hybrid who ended the curse. The rest is just paperwork.”
She almost laughed. Almost.
But then she felt a pulse from the bond. It was warm. Constant.
She had someone beside her.
“We still have the final step,” she said.
“What’s that?”
“The old law says the king must be marked to a partner of equal blood. Since I’m half-pure and half-hybrid, my Lycan side still needs a consort with the old blood line.”
“I’m Lycan enough,” Ethan said. “My family goes back three thousand years.”
“And will you stand beside me as queen consort?”
Ethan smiled—a real smile, the first she’d seen from him. “I thought you’d never ask.”
She leaned into his shoulder.
Tomorrow, they would bury her mother.
But tonight, she let herself feel hope.

## 第12章 Full Awakening

The funeral was held at dawn.
Hybrids and purebloods stood together in the palace courtyard. The coffin was made of white birch, covered with silver roses. Ella stood at the head, Ethan beside her, Jason and his family in the front row.
The Elder Scarread the funeral rites. Then Ella stepped forward.
She spoke without notes.
“My mother spent ten years in a cell, feeding the curse that was meant to destroy us. She never gave up. She sent me messages. She protected me with her last shred of power. Today, I honor her not with sadness, but with a promise: the world she dreamed of—where no child is judged by blood—I will build it.”
She scattered her mother’s ashes into the wind.
The crowd bowed their heads.
Then, the coronation.
Inside the great hall, the crown was placed on Ella’s head—a diadem of moonstone and silver, forged for the Dawn Wolf.
When she looked up, her eyes glowed silver-white.
A gasp rippled through the crowd.
The full awakening had come.
The hybrid queen stood before them, her aura blazing. Behind her, eight dead kings’ spirits flickered—approving.
From the back of the hall, a woman screamed and collapsed.
When the medics reached her, they found she was a changeling—a pureblood spy planted by a surviving faction of Marcus’s loyalists. Possessed by a blood relic.
Ella walked to the woman, her steps measured. The silver fire spread from her feet, wrapping the changeling in light, burning out the relic without harming the woman.
The woman gasped awake, terrified.
Ella helped her stand. “You’re free. Tell your masters the hybrid’s throne has a cleansing fire now. They will not succeed.”
The woman stumbled away.
Ella turned back to the hall. “We begin again.”
The crowd erupted into cheers.
But amidst the noise, Ella felt a subtle vibration under her feet. A deep pulse of magic, far beneath the palace.
She looked at Ethan. “Do you feel that?”
He nodded.
“There’s something else down there.”
The bond pulled at her—not to the dungeons, but deeper. A sealed crypt.
She would investigate later. For now, she let herself be lifted in the arms of her people.
That night, in the private chamber, she sat with Ethan, the crown removed.
“I felt something tonight.” She told him about the pulse.
“I’ve heard rumors of a relic of the first king. It was said to hold the power to unite all wolves permanently. But it was lost when the bloodline was broken.”
“Maybe it’s not lost.” Ella stared at the floor. “Maybe my father left it.”
“Even if it’s there, the vault hasn’t been opened in five centuries. Only a king or queen of full blood can open it.”
“I’m half. Will that burn me?”
Ethan shook his head. “You’re more than full blood now. The awakening made you whole.”
She nodded, decision made.
“Tomorrow, we find out what my father left behind.”

## 第13章 Father’s Vault

The vault entrance was hidden beneath the throne.
Ella ordered the throne lifted, revealing a stone staircase spiraling down. She carried a torch of silver flame. Ethan followed, plus two guards.
The stairs opened into a circular chamber. In the center, a pedestal of obsidian held a sphere of liquid light.
Words on the wall, in the old tongue: *For the heir who completes the prophecy, the blood of the ancestor shall reveal the peace eternal.*
Ella approached. The sphere hummed.
When she touched it, light flooded her mind.
She saw the First King, her father’s ancestors, the founding of the Lycan nation, and the deal they’d made with the moon spirits to gain the shifting power.
She saw the prophecy, written in the original hand: *A hybrid shall be born of pure and seer. When she marks with the enemy line, the blood feud ends. The dawn wolf shall unite the packs.*
The enemy line. Ethan’s family.
She turned to him, stunned. “Your ancestors and mine were warring houses. The prophecy specifically needed a mark between our bloodlines to seal the unity.”
Ethan’s eyes widened. “That’s why the bond worked instantly. It was destined.”
“We were set up by the moon spirits centuries before we were born.”
The sphere cracked. From within, a silver dagger floated out, handle-first. Ella grasped it. The blade was cool, clear as glass.
“The Unity Blade,” she whispered. “It can bind any pack to the queen’s will, by free consent. It ends forced pack bonds.”
Ella sheathed the blade.
She walked back up the stairs, the weight of history on her shoulders.
Outside, she found Jason waiting with a grim face. “There’s a delegation from the northern packs. They refuse to accept a hybrid queen and are threatening secession.”
“How many packs?”
“Three. About two thousand wolves.”
Ella looked at the Unity Blade. “Then I’ll visit them myself.”
“That could be a trap.”
“I have the blade. And I have my wolves.” She glanced at Ethan. “And I have you.”
He growled softly. “Let them try to stop us.”
They prepared a small convoy to travel north at dawn.
But that night, Ella’s new senses caught a whisper in the wind. A voice she thought she’d lost. *My daughter. You did well. The vault was my last gift. Now live freely.*
Elena’s voice. A goodbye.
Ella closed her eyes.
She was ready for whatever came.

## 第14章 Northern Storms

The northern territory was all snow and pine.
Ella’s convoy arrived at the Frost Pack stronghold in late afternoon. The gates were closed. Armed wolves lined the walls.
Ella dismounted and walked forward alone. Ethan stayed within reach.
“I am Queen Ella, daughter of Aldric and Elena,” she called out. “I come with the Unity Blade. I want to speak.”
A tall, scarred alpha climbed the gate tower. His voice boomed: “You may be queen in the south, but here we do not bow to half-bloods.”
“I’m not asking you to bow. I’m asking you to listen.”
The alpha hesitated. Then he nodded at the gate.
It opened.
Inside, Ella faced the Frost Pack elders in their long hall. They sat in a ring. She placed the Unity Blade in the center.
“This is the forged weapon of the First King. It can bind packs in peace. I came to offer it as a pact—not of chains, but of choice.”
The alpha, whose name was Bjorn, scoffed. “And why would we trust a southern queen whose reign began in blood?”
“Because the blood was my own mother’s sacrifice.” Ella answered. “And because I have nothing to gain by lying. The law changes I started in the capital will apply to all packs. I am here to invite you to join the new Council. As equals.”
Bjorn studied her. “You really believe that.”
“I do.”
The elders murmured.
Bjorn looked at the Unity Blade. Then he stood. “Accepting your offer doesn’t mean I trust you. But I respect that you came alone.”
He took the blade, held it. It glowed.
The magic recognized his pure heart.
He stared at the glow, then passed it to the next elder. One by one, they touched it. The blade accepted them all.
Bjorn turned back to Ella. “The Frost Pack will attend the peace summit. But if you break your word, we will come for you.”
“Understood.”
She left the stronghold with a treaty in hand.
The other two packs fell more easily after that. By the end of the week, all seven northern packs had agreed to the new charter.
Ella returned to the capital.
There was more work—rebuilding the Council, rewriting old laws, healing the divides. But for the first time in her life, she went to bed without looking over her shoulder.
Ethan was waiting.
She told him about the summit, and he held her close.
“The queen of the Lycans,” he murmured.
“And the consort who’s still not used to it.”
“We’ll learn together.”
Outside the window, the moon was full.
The realm was at peace.

## 第15章 The Heir

Three months later.
The capital had transformed. The old banners with pureblood crests had been replaced with a new emblem: a silver wolf with a moon in its chest, encircled by stars.
Ella sat on the throne, not as a figurehead but as an active ruler. Today, she was hearing petitions. Hybrids and purebloods stood together in the hall.
A woman approached, carrying a small bundle. She was a hybrid from the southern villages. She had a case of land theft against a pureblood baron.
Ella listened. She asked questions. She pronounced judgment in favor of the woman.
The baron was forced to pay reparations. The crowd cheered.
Afterward, Ella retired to the private chambers. She was exhausted. But it was the good exhaustion of purpose.
Ethan came in, carrying a tray of tea with honey.
“You look tired.”
“I’m running a kingdom.”
He set the tray down and put his hands on her shoulders, massaging. She leaned into him.
For a moment, neither spoke.
Then she felt a flutter in her core. A presence she hadn’t noticed before.
She went still.
“What?” Ethan asked.
She placed a hand on her stomach. Something had awakened. Something new.
She reached inside—the bond, the visions—and saw a tiny silver spark. A heartbeat, connected to both her and Ethan.
A child.
Her eyes went wide.
Ethan caught her expression. “Ella?”
“I’m pregnant.”
He stared at her, then dropped to his knees, pressing his ear to her belly. “You’re serious.”
“I saw it in a vision just now. A small spark.”
He lifted her up, laughing. “We’re going to have a pup.”
Ella laughed too. The joy was surprising—after all the grief and battle, here was life.
She let herself enjoy it.
At sunset, she stood on the palace balcony, the same view her mother must have seen from her cell. But now it was free.
One year later, Ella gave birth to twins. A boy and a girl. Their eyes—one silver, one gold—mirrored the prophecy.
The realm rejoiced.
On the day of their naming ceremony, the packs came from north and south, hybrid and pureblood, to witness the heirs of the Dawn Wolf.
Ella stood with Ethan, each holding a child.
She looked at the crowd, at the peace she had fought for, at the family she had built.
And from deep in her blood, she heard her mother’s whisper one last time: *You are the dawn, my daughter. And now I can rest.*
Ella smiled.
The hybrid’s revenge was complete. And a new prophecy had begun.
