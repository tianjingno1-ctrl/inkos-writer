# The Hybrid’s Revenge: The Billionaire Lycan’s Secret Prophecy

## 开篇钩子

Ella’s mind went white. She saw the cable snap before it fell—a blur of steel, tearing through the elevator shaft. Her hand shot out without thinking, grabbing the man beside her and yanking him back. The broken cable slammed into the floor where he’d been standing.
Ethan Blackwood turned. His gaze swept over her. “You moved fast.”
She let go of his sleeve, heart hammering. “I just—I saw it.”
He didn’t say anything else. But as he stepped out on the top floor, his hand tightened around his palm. Blood beaded between his fingers—a wound that hadn’t been there before.
Ella walked home in the rain, rubbing her arm where a new burn seared under her sleeve. She didn’t dare look.
That night, she dreamed of a woman in chains who whispered her name.

## 第1章 Traces of Prophecy

Ella Vance kept her head down as the three pureblood women walked past her desk.
“Shift your chair, half-breed. You’re blocking the light.” Cassie’s coffee cup tipped deliberately. Warm liquid splashed across Ella’s keyboard and dripped onto her skirt. The other two laughed.
Ella counted to five. She’d learned to count. It kept her hands from shaking.
She blotted the spill with napkins, said nothing. Behind her, she heard someone whisper “pathetic.” The word lodged in her chest like a splinter.
At five thirty, she packed her bag and walked to the elevator. Her skirt was still damp. The workday had been long—another meeting where Lydia Vance, her so-called cousin and head of accounting, had suggested she was “better suited for a role without client contact.”
Ella had smiled. She always smiled.
The elevator doors opened. She stepped inside. A man in a dark suit followed. Ethan Blackwood, CEO of Redwood Holdings. He was taller up close, with eyes the color of warm earth. He pressed a button and stood beside her.
The car began to rise.
Then her vision ripped open.
Steel screaming. Sparks bursting. A cable whipping like a snake. She saw the floor number change—3, 4—and the ceiling crack. In her sight, the cable would come down right where he stood.
She lunged. Her fingers closed around his sleeve and dragged him sideways. A second later, the cable punched through the floor panel, screeching as it coiled where he’d been.
Silence. Then his voice, low and calm. “You just saved my life.”
Ella’s legs shook. “I don’t know why I did that. I saw—I thought I saw something.”
He looked at her. His gaze was steady, measuring. “It happens sometimes. A flash of danger. Some people call it instinct.”
He didn’t mention her eyes. He didn’t mention silver. She felt a flicker of relief.
The elevator reached the top floor. He stepped out, then paused. “Good night, Miss Vance.”
She stayed inside until the doors closed.
When she finally got home, she locked the door and collapsed on her bed. Her right arm burned. She pushed up her sleeve and stared.
A silver mark coiled around her forearm, delicate as frost. She touched it. Pain flared.
In the bathroom mirror, her eyes were normal. Brown. But for an instant—one heartbeat—they turned silver.
She knocked a glass off the counter. It shattered. When she looked again, the silver was gone.
Her phone buzzed. Unknown number: *You are not what they think. Be careful.*
She dropped the phone as if burned.
That night, she didn’t sleep. She kept the lights on and watched the shadows.

## 第2章 The Promotion Trap

Ella arrived at work the next morning to find the office buzzing.
Cassie blocked her path, sneering. “Looks like someone special got an upgrade. Private assistant to the CEO. Congratulations, half-breed.”
Ella’s stomach dropped. The email had come at six a.m. She hadn’t applied for it.
Lydia appeared behind Cassie, clicking across the marble floor. Her eyes were ice. “This is a mistake. She’s a temp with no qualifications.”
Ethan Blackwood’s voice cut through from the elevator. “The decision is made. Ella, with me.”
She grabbed her bag and walked toward him. The whole floor watched.
In the private elevator, she said, “I didn’t ask for this.”
“You didn’t need to.” He pressed a button. “You have potential. I want to see where it goes.”
She frowned. Potential? He didn’t explain.
The top floor office was wide and glass-walled. She noticed a file open on his desk—a photograph of a woman with silver-streaked hair. Her mother.
Ella’s chest tightened. “Who is that?”
Ethan closed the folder. “Someone from a past life. Don’t worry about it.”
He assigned her a desk in the outer office. She sorted files, answered calls. At midday, she went down to the archive room to pull old contracts.
As she reached for a box, the vision came again. Flames. Smoke. A fire alarm. She saw the sprinklers fail, the stairwell fill with heat.
She ran out of the archive room and found two janitors near a faulty electrical panel, sparks flying. She pulled them away. Moments later, the panel caught fire.
Ethan arrived as the sprinklers finally kicked on. He looked at her. “You have a talent for being in the right place.”
She said nothing. But she caught Lydia watching from the hallway, suspicion in her eyes.
That afternoon, Ella found a manila envelope on her desk. Inside: a copy of her birth certificate, stamped with a mark she didn’t recognize. A handwritten note: *We know what you are. Leave before we take action.*
She shoved it into her bag.
On her way out, she passed Lydia’s office. The door was open. Lydia was on the phone, voice low: “...she triggered something today. I’m sending my report tonight. Yes, the Council needs to know.”
Ella walked faster.
At home, she locked the door and slid to the floor. The silver mark on her arm was pulsing. She hadn’t even told Ethan about the note.
In the dark, she heard a whisper. *Daughter. Stay alive. They are coming.*
She jerked her head up. No one was there.
But on her bed lay a single white rose, fresh, as if just picked. No note. No explanation.
She didn’t sleep. She kept the rose in her hand.

## 第3章 The First Move

Three days passed. Ella worked under Ethan’s wing, learning the business. He taught her nothing about the supernatural. He simply showed her how to read contracts and manage his calendar.
But she felt him watching. Testing.
On the fourth day, she stayed late. The parking lot was empty when she left the building.
Three men stepped out from behind a truck. Pureblood enforcers. She recognized the emblem on their jackets: the Vance family crest.
“Ella Vance,” the leader said, rolling his shoulders. “We have orders to collect a sample. Routine check. If you’re clean, we’ll let you go.”
She backed up. Her heart slammed against her ribs. A sample meant they’d draw blood—and if they sensed the hybrid taint, they’d drag her to the Council.
Her arm burned. The silver mark was hidden under her sleeve, but she felt it pulsing.
“I’m an employee of Redwood. You have no jurisdiction here.”
“We have every jurisdiction. The Council supersedes corporate policy.”
The leader stepped forward, reaching for her arm.
Then a shot cracked the air. A bullet hit the ground at his feet.
Ethan appeared from the shadows, a smoking gun in his hand. “She’s under my protection.”
The enforcer froze. “Mr. Blackwood. This doesn’t concern you.”
“It does now.” Ethan’s voice was ice. “The Council has no authority over my private property. And she is company property. You want her blood? Get a warrant.”
The enforcer snarled but backed away. “This isn’t over.”
They left.
Ella sagged against the wall. Ethan grabbed her arm and pulled her toward his car. “We need to talk.”
He drove to a diner outside the city. In a booth, he slid a folder across the table. Inside: her birth certificate, a photo of her mother, and a page from an old prophecy.
“Your mother was the Seer of the Lycan royal house,” he said. “Your father was King Aldric. My family was killed by the same people who killed him—the Elder Council. They want me dead because I’m the last of the Northern Lycan line. And they want you because your blood can power the blood curse that enslaves hybrids.”
She stared at him. “Why are you telling me this now?”
“Because I wanted to see if you could handle it. The other day, you saw the cable. You saw the fire. You have the gift. But you don’t control it yet.”
“And you? What do you want from me?”
“I want to stop the Council. But I can’t do it alone.” He leaned forward. “I can train you. Teach you to control your visions. In return, you help me take them down.”
“And if I refuse?”
“They’ll find you. And they’ll use you to kill every hybrid alive. Including yourself.”
Ella wrapped her hands around her coffee cup. Her reflection wavered in the dark liquid.
“I’m in,” she said. “But I don’t trust you.”
“That’s fine. Trust will come later.”

## 第4章 Escape

The next night, two assassins broke into Ella’s apartment.
She woke to the sound of glass breaking. A shadow lunged for her, knife gleaming. Her vision flashed—she saw the arc of the blade a second before it swung. She rolled off the bed, grabbing a lamp to block.
The second assassin came from behind. She ducked and ran for the door.
Ethan crashed through the window, already shifted into a half-Lycan form. He tore through one assassin, grabbed her hand, and pulled her down the fire escape.
They reached his car and sped away.
“They found you too fast,” he said. “Someone in my company is feeding them information.”
He drove to a safe house in the woods. A two-story cabin, warded with silver.
Ella collapsed on the couch. Her hands wouldn’t stop shaking.
Ethan brought her a glass of water. “You used your vision.”
“I saw the knife. A second before.”
“Good. That’s the first stage. But you can’t rely on instinct. You need to train.”
She nodded, but her mind was elsewhere. “I keep hearing a woman’s voice. In my head.”
Ethan’s expression shifted. “Describe her.”
“She says she’s my mother. She called my name. She told me they’re keeping her underground.”
Ethan sat down heavily. “That’s your mother. She’s alive—in the palace dungeons. The Council keeps her there to drain her seer power.”
Ella’s throat closed. “Why didn’t you tell me?”
“Because you weren’t ready to hear it and still think clearly.”
She stood up. “I’m ready now. We need to go get her.”
“No. Not yet. The Blood Moon is in ten days. That’s when the Council will try to activate the curse using her as the sacrifice. If we move before that, they’ll kill her.”
Ella’s hands balled into fists. “Ten days. That’s how long I have to get strong enough.”
“Yes. And to learn to trust me.”
She met his eyes. “That depends on what you’re not telling me.”
He held her gaze. “Fair.”
That night, she lay in the guest room, alone. A white rose appeared on the pillow.
She pressed it to her chest and cried.

## 第5章 The Limit of Vision

Training began at dawn.
Ethan led her into the clearing behind the cabin. “The sight works on threat detection. You see danger a second or two before it happens. But you can’t control what you see. You can only react. We’re going to change that.”
He attacked her without warning. She dodged the first punch, thanks to a flash. The second caught her shoulder. She stumbled.
“Again.”
Hour after hour. By noon, she was bruised and bleeding. But she could see two seconds ahead—sometimes three.
He stopped and handed her a towel. “Good progress. But there’s a cost. Every use drains your energy. If you push too hard, you’ll pass out.”
She wiped her face. “How do I last longer?”
“You need to bond with a Lycan partner. The bond shares your energy load. But that’s a permanent decision.”
She looked at him. “You mean mark each other.”
“Yes. It’s not something to do lightly.”
She said nothing.
That afternoon, she practiced alone. She tried to trigger visions on command. She focused on the tree in front of her. She willed herself to see it fall. Nothing.
Then she thought of her mother. The vision came: a stone cell, a woman with silver hair, chains. She heard a whisper: *Come to the Blood Moon. Bring the king’s blood.*
Ella collapsed, head pounding.
Ethan found her. “You forced it.”
“I saw her. She told me to come.”
“The Blood Moon is eight days away. You’re not ready.”
“I have to try.”
“You won’t survive. Not alone.”
She looked at him. “Then teach me the bond.”
He held her eyes for a long moment. “You don’t trust me.”
“No. But I’ll risk it if it saves her.”
He stepped closer. “The bond isn’t a transaction. If we do it, we do it fully. Or it won’t work.”
She swallowed. “What does that mean?”
“It means we have to mean it. Both of us. If either of us hesitates, the mark will burn out and we’ll both go mad.”
She thought about the past week. The way he’d saved her. The way he looked at her. The way her heart beat when he was near.
“I don’t know if I can mean it yet.”
“Then we wait.”
He walked away.
That night, she stared at the ceiling. The silver mark on her arm pulsed. She wanted to save her mother. She wanted to live. She wanted Ethan to look at her the way he’d looked at her when she’d said no.
Maybe she already meant it.

## 第6章 The Diary

On the fifth day, Ethan gave her a worn leather book. “Your mother’s diary. I retrieved it years ago.”
Ella took it with trembling hands. She opened it.
*To my child, if you ever read this—know that Marcus Vance is not your uncle by blood alone. He killed your father. He chained me in the depths to feed the blood curse. The only way to break it is to merge the two bloodlines: my foresight and your father’s Lycan kingship. That can only happen through a true love bond. Not a contract. Truth.*
Ella looked up. “She said the bond must be true.”
Ethan was leaning against the wall. “I know.”
“Then you and I... we can’t fake it.”
“No. And that’s why I didn’t push you.”
She closed the diary. “I need to know why you’re really doing this. You said your family was killed. But you never told me who.”
Ethan’s face went hard. “The Elder Council. Led by Marcus Vance. They burned our estate when I was twelve. My parents, my sister—I was the only survivor. I swore I’d burn them all.”
“So this is revenge for you.”
“Yes. For you, it’s family.”
She held his gaze. “Then we have the same goal.”
“But not the same reason.”
“Does it matter?”
He took a step closer. “Yes. Because the bond needs more than shared enemy. It needs shared trust.”
They stood in silence.
Then Ella said, “I want to try.”
“The bond?”
“Yes. I want to trust you. I want to be strong enough to save her. Show me what to do.”
He took her hand. “It starts with a cut. Our blood against each other.”
They sat on the floor. He drew a knife and cut her palm, then his own. He pressed their hands together.
The world shuddered.
She saw fragments: a boy hiding in a closet while flames consumed his home. A man in his twenties swearing over graves. A shadow of her own face reflected in his eyes.
She felt his loss. And she felt his hope—in her.
When they broke apart, both were breathing hard.
“Did it work?” she asked.
He looked at his hand. A thin silver scar glowed across his palm. “It started. It’s not complete yet. But it started.”
She looked at her own hand. A matching scar.
From somewhere deep, she heard her mother’s voice: *Good. Now come.*
The Blood Moon was three days away.

## 第7章 Blood Moon Infiltration

They moved at dusk.
Ethan had forged documents to enter the palace under the guise of Council suppliers. Ella wore a hooded cloak to hide her face.
The palace was vast, carved from black stone. Torches lined the walls. They passed through the outer halls without incident—the guards were focused on the Blood Moon preparations in the central courtyard.
But as they reached the lower chambers, a figure blocked the passage.
Lydia Vance. Dressed in ceremonial robes. A cruel smile on her lips.
“I knew you’d come, cousin. You’re as predictable as your mother.”
Ella tensed. “She’s not your cousin.”
“She’s a traitor.” Lydia gestured, and a dozen armed guards emerged from side corridors. “Seize them.”
Ethan shifted—his body grew, claws extended. He tore through three guards before the others could react.
Ella used her vision to dodge a strike and grabbed a fallen guard’s dagger.
But Lydia was faster. She threw a blast of dark energy that sent Ella crashing into the wall.
“You think you can challenge the Council?” Lydia sneered. “You’re a half-breed whose only trick is seeing a few seconds ahead.”
Ella struggled to her feet. Her arm burned. The silver mark was glowing.
Then a voice echoed in her mind: *Daughter. Draw the moon’s power. Strike her with light.*
Ella raised her hand. She concentrated on the moon—the Blood Moon rising outside. A beam of silver light lanced from her palm, hitting Lydia’s chest.
Lydia stumbled back, robes smoking. “What—?”
Ethan finished off the last guard and grabbed Ella. “We don’t have much time. The ritual is starting.”
They ran down the stairs. The altar chamber was ahead.
Inside, the High Priest stood over a stone altar where Elena lay chained, pale and weak.
Marcus Vance stood at the head, a dark blade in his hand.
“Welcome, niece. You’re just in time for the final act.”
Ella charged.
But Marcus raised his hand, and a wave of force threw her back. She landed hard, her vision flickering.
Ethan lunged at Marcus, but the King’s guard intercepted—Jason, Ethan’s own second-in-command, holding a silver blade.
“I’m sorry, boss,” Jason said, face pained. “They have my family.”
Ethan gaped. “Jason?”
But Jason didn’t attack. He turned and stabbed the guard beside him.
“I’m not with them.” He threw Ethan a sword. “Go. I’ll hold the door.”
Ethan caught the sword and turned on Marcus.
The fight was brutal. Marcus was centuries old, fueled by stolen power. Ethan was younger, but his Lycan blood was pure.
Ella rose and ran to the altar. She pushed the chains, but they were enchanted.
“Mother.”
Elena opened her eyes. “You came. But you must flee. He is too strong.”
“No. I’m not leaving you.”
Elena’s hand touched Ella’s cheek. “The bond—you started it. You need to finish it. Only the full bond can give you the power to stop him.”
“With Ethan?”
“Yes. Now. While there is time.”
Ella looked back at Ethan fighting Marcus. He was bleeding. His arm was cut deep.
She reached through the bond. *Ethan. I’m ready. Do it now.*
He didn’t answer, but she felt his awareness. He threw a punch to create distance, then turned.
Marcus took the opening—a dagger of dark energy slammed into Ethan’s chest.
He dropped.
“Ethan!” Ella’s scream tore through the chamber.

## 第8章 The Bond

Ella threw herself at Marcus, a wave of silver fire erupting from her hands. He blocked, surprised by her strength. But she wasn’t aiming for him—she grabbed Ethan and pulled him behind the altar.
Ethan’s breath was shallow. Blood pooled under his chest.
“You’re going to be fine,” she said, tears streaming. “You have to be fine.”
He smiled weakly. “Finish it. The bond—our blood.”
She cut her palm again, pressed it to his. “I promise. I trust you. I—”
She couldn’t say it. But she kissed him.
The mark flared. Power rushed through her.
She felt his life force draining, but she poured her own into him—her vision energy, her hybrid blood. The wound began to close.
Ethan’s eyes snapped open. The iris caught fire—gold and silver. He roared, shifting fully into a massive black wolf.
Marcus stepped back. “Impossible. The mark—it’s complete?”
Ella stood, the silver flame wreathing her body. “It’s over, Uncle.”
Jason had cleared the remaining guards and was helping Elena off the altar.
Marcus laughed. “You think a bond makes you strong? Let me show you true power.”
He raised his hands. The altar chamber shook. The Blood Moon above bled into a black eclipse.
Elena screamed. “He’s activating the curse now—he’ll drain all hybrids!”
Ella looked at Ethan. He was huge, but he was hurting.
“Together,” she said.
He nodded.
They attacked as one. Ella’s silver flame and Ethan’s wolf strength. Marcus deflected them, but they kept pressing.
Ella saw his moves a second ahead. She called out directions to Ethan.
They forced Marcus back to the edge of the altar platform.
Then Marcus made a mistake. He overextended his shield.
Ella poured all her energy into a single spear of light. It pierced him through the chest.
Marcus stared, disbelieving. “You—you can’t—I am eternal—”
He crumpled.
The black eclipse shattered. The Blood Moon returned to normal.
And in the distance, the curse’s chains broke. Hybrids across the realm felt it.
Ella fell to her knees. Elena caught her.
“You did it, daughter.”
Ella turned, sobbing. “You’re alive.”
Elena smiled. “Yes. Now we need to go—the Council’s death throes will bring more enemies.”
Ethan limped over, human again. “She’s right. We need to secure the palace.”
Ella looked at her mother, then at Ethan.
“We do it together.”

## 第9章 The Rebellion Forms

The palace fell into chaos. News of Marcus Vance’s death spread quickly. Pureblood loyalists regrouped, and hybrid prisoners rose up.
Ella and Ethan set up a command post in the throne room. Jason guarded Elena, who was weak but conscious.
A messenger arrived: “The northern packs refuse to accept a hybrid queen. They’re marching south to reclaim the throne.”
Ella inhaled sharply. “How long?”
“Three days. They have two thousand wolves.”
Ethan turned to Ella. “We can’t fight them all. We need allies.”
“The hybrids are our allies.”
“They’re unarmed. We need pureblood defectors.”
Ella looked at the throne. Marcus’s body still lay at the base. She made a decision.
“Broadcast to every stronghold. Hybrid and pureblood. I challenge any alpha who contests my rule to single combat. If I lose, they can have the crown. If I win, they swear allegiance.”
Ethan grabbed her arm. “That’s suicide. You’re not fully healed.”
“It’s our only chance.”
Elena spoke weakly. “She’s right. The old law allows challenges. If she wins, the challenge binds them.”
Ella met Ethan’s eyes. “I won’t lose.”
He held her gaze, then nodded.
The broadcast went out.
That night, a delegation of northern alphas arrived. Their leader was Bjorn, a scarred warrior twice her size.
“So you’re the hybrid queen,” he sneered. “You don’t look like much.”
“Looks can deceive.”
“We’ll see. Tomorrow at dawn. Single combat. No weapons, no magic—just the wolf. Then we’ll see who rules.”
Ella agreed.
All night, she trained with Ethan. She was exhausted, but she couldn’t afford to show weakness.
At dawn, she faced Bjorn in the courtyard. Hundreds watched.
Bjorn shifted into a massive brown wolf.
Ella shifted too—but her form wasn’t pure black. It was silver and white, streaked with light. The Dawn Wolf.
Bjorn paused, surprised.
They clashed. Bjorn was strong, but Ella’s vision let her see each of his moves before he made them. She dodged, feinted, struck when he was off balance.
After fifteen minutes, she pinned him, jaws at his throat.
“Yield.”
Bjorn’s eyes flickered. Then he placed his head on the ground.
“I yield.”
The crowd erupted.
Ella released him, shifted back, offered her hand. “Rise, Alpha. We fight together now.”
Bjorn took her hand. “You have my pack.”
That day, the northern packs allied with the hybrids. The rebellion became an army.
Ella stood on the palace balcony, Ethan beside her.
“We’re not done yet,” she said. “The Council still has cells around the kingdom. And there’s something else—my mother’s prophecy said there’s a relic of the first king that can unite all wolves forever. If we find it, no one can challenge us.”
“Where is it?”
“Under the throne. My father left it.”
Ethan looked at her. “When do we go?”
“Now.”
They walked back inside, ready for the next battle.

## 第10章 The Vault

The throne room was empty. The guards had been dismissed for a ceasefire meeting. Ella and Ethan lifted the stone slab hidden beneath the carpet.
A spiral staircase descended into darkness.
They climbed down, torches lighting the way. After two hundred steps, they reached a circular chamber.
At its center, a pedestal of obsidian held a sphere of liquid light.
Ancient words were carved on the wall: *For the heir who completes the prophecy, the blood of the ancestor shall reveal the peace eternal.*
Ella touched the sphere.
The room dissolved.
She saw the First King founding the Lycan nation. She saw the prophecy written in silver: *A hybrid shall be born of pure and seer. When she marks with the enemy line, the blood feud ends. The dawn wolf shall unite the packs.*
The enemy line. Ethan’s family.
She turned. “The prophecy says our families were enemies. They fought for centuries. The only way to unite the packs was for a hybrid from my line to bond with someone from yours.”
Ethan’s face went pale. “So the bond—it was prophecy.”
“It was destiny.”
The sphere cracked. A dagger emerged, its blade clear as glass. The Unity Blade, it was called. It could bind any pack to the queen by free consent.
Ella sheathed it.
They returned upstairs.
But as they stepped into the throne room, they found chaos.
Guards running. Jason met them at the door, blood on his cheek. “The Elder Council’s loyalists launched a surprise attack. They’re trying to take the palace back.”
Ethan snarled. “How many?”
“Five hundred. With a siege engine.”
Ella looked at the blade. “Then we use this.”
She rallied the hybrid and northern wolves. They formed a shield wall at the palace gates.
The loyalists charged.
Ella stood at the front. “I am the queen marked by prophecy. I carry the Unity Blade. If you fight me, you fight the will of the moon.”
The loyalists hesitated.
Then their commander screamed, “She’s a half-breed! Attack!”
They broke rank and charged.
The battle was fierce. Ella cut down five with the blade—it shimmered, and each one she struck fell paralyzed, not dead.
More wolves joined her. The tide turned.
The loyalist commander fell to Ethan’s claws. The rest surrendered.
In the silence after, Ella stood covered in blood, but none of it was hers.
She held the blade high. “This is the weapon that binds. Tomorrow, I will rewrite the law. No more blood-based privileges. Hybrid and pureblood stand equal.”
The cheer that went up shook the walls.

## 第11章 The Underground Resistance

But peace didn’t last.
A week later, a new threat emerged. A faction of pureblood extremists fled underground, taking a cache of dark artifacts. They began attacking hybrid settlements.
Ella sent scouts. They found the hideout in an abandoned mine.
She decided to lead the raid herself.
“You can’t go,” Ethan argued. “You’re the queen. If you fall, everything unravels.”
“If I don’t go, they’ll think I’m hiding behind a crown.”
She took a squad of ten, including Jason and Bjorn.
They entered the mine. Dark magic hung in the air.
The extremists had set traps—explosive runes, poisoned gas. Ella’s vision saved them three times.
At the core, they found a chamber filled with blood relics. The leader was a woman named Vera, an old ally of Marcus.
“You killed my master,” Vera spat. “But his legacy lives on.”
She activated a relic. Shadows rose, forming monstrous shapes.
The squad fought. Ella used the Unity Blade—it cut through shadows, but they reformed.
She needed light.
She closed her eyes and drew from the bond. Silver fire erupted, burning the shadows away.
Vera screamed as the fire consumed the relics.
The surviving extremists surrendered.
Ella walked to Vera. “Your master is dead. His legacy ends now. You will stand trial, and if you repent, you may live.”
Vera spat at her feet.
Ella wiped it calmly. “Take her away.”
Back at the palace, Ella sat in her chambers, staring at the blade.
“I’m tired of fighting.”
Ethan came beside her. “It’s not over yet. The underground network is large. But we have momentum.”
“I want to see my mother.”
Elena was recovering in a wing of the palace. Ella visited her.
Elena was still weak, but her eyes were brighter. “You’re doing well, daughter.”
“I’m just reacting. I want to build something lasting.”
“You will. But first, you need to secure the borders. The northern packs are loyal, but the western packs have not yet submitted.”
Ella nodded. “I’ll send emissaries. And if they refuse, I’ll go myself.”
“That’s my daughter.”
Ella hugged her mother. For a moment, she let herself feel hope.

## 第12章 The Western Pack

The western packs were a loose alliance of three clans, led by a council of alphas. They had refused Ella’s invitation to the peace summit.
Ella traveled with a small retinue: Ethan, Jason, and Bjorn. The journey took two days.
The meeting was tense. The alphas were old, pureblood, steeped in tradition.
“You may have united the north,” said their leader, a grizzled wolf named Orin. “But we do not bow to prophecy and half-blood queens.”
Ella placed the Unity Blade on the table. “I’m not asking you to bow. I’m asking you to join. If you don’t, you’ll be isolated—cut off from trade, treaties, and protection.”
Orin’s eyes narrowed. “Are you threatening us?”
“I’m stating reality.”
One of the younger alphas stood. “I say we challenge her. Prove if her prophecy is real.”
Orin considered. “One fight. If you win, we discuss terms. If you lose, you leave our territory forever.”
Ella agreed.
She faced the young alpha, a man named Rune. He shifted into a black wolf with a white chest.
She didn’t shift. She drew the Unity Blade. “I’ll use this. It won’t kill, but it will bind.”
Rune laughed—then charged.
Ella sidestepped his first lunge, slashed the blade across his leg. He stumbled. She disarmed him with a twist of the blade.
He hit the ground, paralyzed.
She stood over him. “Yield.”
He growled, but Orin raised a hand. “Enough.”
Orin looked at the blade. “I didn’t believe the relics still existed. You have the unity weapon. That proves something.” He exhaled. “We will come to the summit. But we will not swear allegiance. Not yet.”
“That’s enough for now.”
Ella sheathed the blade.
They returned home with a tentative alliance.
But news came as they arrived: the underground extremists were planning a final strike on the capital. They had captured a group of hybrid children to use as hostages.
Ella’s blood went cold.
“We have to save them.”
“We will,” Ethan said. “But we need a plan.”

## 第13章 Hostage Crisis

The extremists demanded a trade: the Unity Blade for the twelve children.
Ella refused to give up the blade. Instead, she decided to infiltrate.
She went alone, at night, using her visions to avoid patrols. She found the children in a locked warehouse, guarded by five armed men.
She waited until the guards changed shift, then struck. She took down two before the alarm was raised. A fight broke out.
Ethan and Jason arrived as backup—they’d tracked her.
The extremists fought hard. One grabbed a child and pressed a dagger to her throat.
“Back off, or she dies!”
Ella froze. Her vision showed her three outcomes: she could throw the blade and kill him, but the child might be cut. She could rush, but the blade would strike.
She had to trust her speed.
She took a breath, then threw the Unity Blade—not at the man, but at the lamp above him.
The lamp shattered, plunging the room into darkness.
In the dark, she moved. Her vision guided her hand. She grabbed the child, pulled her away, and knocked the man out.
When the lights came back, the children were free.
The extremists were captured.
Ella held the smallest child, a five-year-old girl with silver eyes. “You’re safe now.”
The girl smiled.
Back at the palace, the people heard of the rescue. Ella’s popularity soared.
Even the western alphas sent word: they would attend the summit.
Ella felt a shift. The war was not over, but the tide had turned.
That night, Ethan found her on the balcony.
“You were brave tonight.”
“I was scared.” She laughed shakily. “But it worked.”
“Because you have the bond. We have each other.”
She leaned into him. “I think I’m ready to finish it. Officially. The binding ceremony.”
“You mean the royal marking?”
“Yes. I want the whole kingdom to know we’re bound. Not just prophecy—choice.”
Ethan took her hand. “Say when.”
“After the summit. We’ll do it then.”
Below, the city was lit with candles and celebration.
For the first time, she felt like a queen.

## 第14章 The Summit

The Western Summit began at noon. Delegates from every pack arrived. The hall was crowded.
Ella stood on the dais. “I welcome you as free wolves. We are here to rewrite the pact that has governed us for a thousand years.”
She laid out the new charter: all wolves equal under the law, hybrid representation on the Council, abolition of blood-based privilege.
Debate raged for hours. Some alphas agreed quickly. Others argued.
But when the final vote came, the charter passed.
The hybrid and pureblood representatives signed it together.
Ella signed last, sealing it with a drop of her blood.
The crowd cheered.
Afterward, she and Ethan stood in the hall as the celebration began.
“We did it,” she said.
“We did.” He pulled her close. “There’s still work. But this is a beginning.”
“Tomorrow, we do the bonding ceremony. I want everyone to see.”
“Are you sure?”
“I’ve never been more sure.”
That night, she visited her mother’s room. Elena was sitting up, stronger.
“I heard about the summit. You’ve done what I couldn’t.”
“You would have done the same.”
Elena smiled. “Tomorrow, when you bond, I want to be there.”
“You will.”
Ella hugged her mother.
The next morning, the palace courtyard was prepared. A priest of the moon stood ready. The entire court gathered.
Ella wore a white gown, silver embroidery tracing the pattern of the mark on her arm.
Ethan wore a black suit with a silver tie.
They faced each other. The priest spoke the words.
“Do you, Ella Vance, take Ethan Blackwood as your true bonded, under the witness of the moon, for life and beyond?”
“I do.”
“Do you, Ethan Blackwood, take Ella Vance as your true bonded, under the witness of the moon, for life and beyond?”
“I do.”
They cut their palms. Pressed together. The mark on their arms flared bright.
The bond completed.
The wolves in the audience howled.
Ella felt the power settle. She was stronger. She was whole.
She and Ethan kissed.
As they pulled back, she felt a strange flutter in her core—something new.
She placed a hand on her stomach.
Ethan noticed. “What is it?”
“I don’t know. A feeling.”
She looked out at the crowd. At the future.
“We’ll have time to find out,” she said.

## 第15章 New Dawn

Six months passed.
The new council was running smoothly. Hybrids served as judges and generals. The old laws were dismantled piece by piece.
Ella’s belly swelled. She was pregnant—a child from the bonding. The prophecy had hinted at it, but she hadn’t believed until the healers confirmed.
Elena stayed in the palace, regaining strength to guide her daughter through the reigns.
One evening, Ella stood on the balcony, Ethan beside her.
“Are you afraid?” he asked.
“Of childbirth? Yes. Of the world we’re building? No.”
“The extremists are still out there. They tried another attack last week.”
“We stopped them. We’ll keep stopping them.”
Ethan nodded.
She looked at the moon. It was full, silver light spilling over the city.
“I used to dream of a life like this,” she said. “A life where I didn’t have to hide. Now I have it.”
“You earned it.”
“We earned it.”
She felt movement in her belly. A flutter.
“Our child is strong.”
“He or she will be the first of a new generation. True equal.”
Ella smiled. “The prophecy ends with us. No more blood feuds. No more curses.”
They stood in silence, watching the moon.
Then a knock came. Jason appeared. “Queen Ella, the emissary from the eastern coast has arrived. They want to join the charter.”
Ella sighed, pleased. “Let them in. Tomorrow we will conduct the ceremony.”
She turned to go inside, but paused for a final look at the sky.
*Thank you, Mother. Thank you for the prophecy.*
*Thank you for never giving up.*
She walked inside, hand in hand with Ethan, toward a kingdom at peace.
And in her heart, she knew: the hybrid’s revenge had become something greater—a legacy of unity and hope.
