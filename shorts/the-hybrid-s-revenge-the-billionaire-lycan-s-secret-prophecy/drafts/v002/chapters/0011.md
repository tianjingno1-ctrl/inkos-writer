# 第11章 The Underground Resistance

But peace didn’t last.
A week later, a new threat emerged. A faction of pureblood extremists fled underground, taking a cache of dark artifacts. They began attacking hybrid settlements.
Ella sent scouts. They found the hideout in an abandoned mine.
She decided to lead the raid herself.
“You can’t go,” Ethan argued. “You’re the queen. If you fall, everything unravels.”
“If I don’t go, they’ll think I’m hiding behind a crown.”
She took a squad of ten, including Jason and Bjorn.
They entered the mine. Dark magic hung in the air.
The extremists had set traps—explosive runes, poisoned gas. Ella’s vision saved them three times.
At the core, they found a chamber filled with blood relics. The leader was a woman named Vera, an old ally of Marcus.
“You killed my master,” Vera spat. “But his legacy lives on.”
She activated a relic. Shadows rose, forming monstrous shapes.
The squad fought. Ella used the Unity Blade—it cut through shadows, but they reformed.
She needed light.
She closed her eyes and drew from the bond. Silver fire erupted, burning the shadows away.
Vera screamed as the fire consumed the relics.
The surviving extremists surrendered.
Ella walked to Vera. “Your master is dead. His legacy ends now. You will stand trial, and if you repent, you may live.”
Vera spat at her feet.
Ella wiped it calmly. “Take her away.”
Back at the palace, Ella sat in her chambers, staring at the blade.
“I’m tired of fighting.”
Ethan came beside her. “It’s not over yet. The underground network is large. But we have momentum.”
“I want to see my mother.”
Elena was recovering in a wing of the palace. Ella visited her.
Elena was still weak, but her eyes were brighter. “You’re doing well, daughter.”
“I’m just reacting. I want to build something lasting.”
“You will. But first, you need to secure the borders. The northern packs are loyal, but the western packs have not yet submitted.”
Ella nodded. “I’ll send emissaries. And if they refuse, I’ll go myself.”
“That’s my daughter.”
Ella hugged her mother. For a moment, she let herself feel hope.
