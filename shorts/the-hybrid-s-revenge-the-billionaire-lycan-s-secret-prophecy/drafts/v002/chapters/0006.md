# 第6章 The Diary

On the fifth day, Ethan gave her a worn leather book. “Your mother’s diary. I retrieved it years ago.”
Ella took it with trembling hands. She opened it.
*To my child, if you ever read this—know that Marcus Vance is not your uncle by blood alone. He killed your father. He chained me in the depths to feed the blood curse. The only way to break it is to merge the two bloodlines: my foresight and your father’s Lycan kingship. That can only happen through a true love bond. Not a contract. Truth.*
Ella looked up. “She said the bond must be true.”
Ethan was leaning against the wall. “I know.”
“Then you and I... we can’t fake it.”
“No. And that’s why I didn’t push you.”
She closed the diary. “I need to know why you’re really doing this. You said your family was killed. But you never told me who.”
Ethan’s face went hard. “The Elder Council. Led by Marcus Vance. They burned our estate when I was twelve. My parents, my sister—I was the only survivor. I swore I’d burn them all.”
“So this is revenge for you.”
“Yes. For you, it’s family.”
She held his gaze. “Then we have the same goal.”
“But not the same reason.”
“Does it matter?”
He took a step closer. “Yes. Because the bond needs more than shared enemy. It needs shared trust.”
They stood in silence.
Then Ella said, “I want to try.”
“The bond?”
“Yes. I want to trust you. I want to be strong enough to save her. Show me what to do.”
He took her hand. “It starts with a cut. Our blood against each other.”
They sat on the floor. He drew a knife and cut her palm, then his own. He pressed their hands together.
The world shuddered.
She saw fragments: a boy hiding in a closet while flames consumed his home. A man in his twenties swearing over graves. A shadow of her own face reflected in his eyes.
She felt his loss. And she felt his hope—in her.
When they broke apart, both were breathing hard.
“Did it work?” she asked.
He looked at his hand. A thin silver scar glowed across his palm. “It started. It’s not complete yet. But it started.”
She looked at her own hand. A matching scar.
From somewhere deep, she heard her mother’s voice: *Good. Now come.*
The Blood Moon was three days away.
