# 第9章 The Rebellion Forms

The palace fell into chaos. News of Marcus Vance’s death spread quickly. Pureblood loyalists regrouped, and hybrid prisoners rose up.
Ella and Ethan set up a command post in the throne room. Jason guarded Elena, who was weak but conscious.
A messenger arrived: “The northern packs refuse to accept a hybrid queen. They’re marching south to reclaim the throne.”
Ella inhaled sharply. “How long?”
“Three days. They have two thousand wolves.”
Ethan turned to Ella. “We can’t fight them all. We need allies.”
“The hybrids are our allies.”
“They’re unarmed. We need pureblood defectors.”
Ella looked at the throne. Marcus’s body still lay at the base. She made a decision.
“Broadcast to every stronghold. Hybrid and pureblood. I challenge any alpha who contests my rule to single combat. If I lose, they can have the crown. If I win, they swear allegiance.”
Ethan grabbed her arm. “That’s suicide. You’re not fully healed.”
“It’s our only chance.”
Elena spoke weakly. “She’s right. The old law allows challenges. If she wins, the challenge binds them.”
Ella met Ethan’s eyes. “I won’t lose.”
He held her gaze, then nodded.
The broadcast went out.
That night, a delegation of northern alphas arrived. Their leader was Bjorn, a scarred warrior twice her size.
“So you’re the hybrid queen,” he sneered. “You don’t look like much.”
“Looks can deceive.”
“We’ll see. Tomorrow at dawn. Single combat. No weapons, no magic—just the wolf. Then we’ll see who rules.”
Ella agreed.
All night, she trained with Ethan. She was exhausted, but she couldn’t afford to show weakness.
At dawn, she faced Bjorn in the courtyard. Hundreds watched.
Bjorn shifted into a massive brown wolf.
Ella shifted too—but her form wasn’t pure black. It was silver and white, streaked with light. The Dawn Wolf.
Bjorn paused, surprised.
They clashed. Bjorn was strong, but Ella’s vision let her see each of his moves before he made them. She dodged, feinted, struck when he was off balance.
After fifteen minutes, she pinned him, jaws at his throat.
“Yield.”
Bjorn’s eyes flickered. Then he placed his head on the ground.
“I yield.”
The crowd erupted.
Ella released him, shifted back, offered her hand. “Rise, Alpha. We fight together now.”
Bjorn took her hand. “You have my pack.”
That day, the northern packs allied with the hybrids. The rebellion became an army.
Ella stood on the palace balcony, Ethan beside her.
“We’re not done yet,” she said. “The Council still has cells around the kingdom. And there’s something else—my mother’s prophecy said there’s a relic of the first king that can unite all wolves forever. If we find it, no one can challenge us.”
“Where is it?”
“Under the throne. My father left it.”
Ethan looked at her. “When do we go?”
“Now.”
They walked back inside, ready for the next battle.
