# 第14章 The Summit

The Western Summit began at noon. Delegates from every pack arrived. The hall was crowded.
Ella stood on the dais. “I welcome you as free wolves. We are here to rewrite the pact that has governed us for a thousand years.”
She laid out the new charter: all wolves equal under the law, hybrid representation on the Council, abolition of blood-based privilege.
Debate raged for hours. Some alphas agreed quickly. Others argued.
But when the final vote came, the charter passed.
The hybrid and pureblood representatives signed it together.
Ella signed last, sealing it with a drop of her blood.
The crowd cheered.
Afterward, she and Ethan stood in the hall as the celebration began.
“We did it,” she said.
“We did.” He pulled her close. “There’s still work. But this is a beginning.”
“Tomorrow, we do the bonding ceremony. I want everyone to see.”
“Are you sure?”
“I’ve never been more sure.”
That night, she visited her mother’s room. Elena was sitting up, stronger.
“I heard about the summit. You’ve done what I couldn’t.”
“You would have done the same.”
Elena smiled. “Tomorrow, when you bond, I want to be there.”
“You will.”
Ella hugged her mother.
The next morning, the palace courtyard was prepared. A priest of the moon stood ready. The entire court gathered.
Ella wore a white gown, silver embroidery tracing the pattern of the mark on her arm.
Ethan wore a black suit with a silver tie.
They faced each other. The priest spoke the words.
“Do you, Ella Vance, take Ethan Blackwood as your true bonded, under the witness of the moon, for life and beyond?”
“I do.”
“Do you, Ethan Blackwood, take Ella Vance as your true bonded, under the witness of the moon, for life and beyond?”
“I do.”
They cut their palms. Pressed together. The mark on their arms flared bright.
The bond completed.
The wolves in the audience howled.
Ella felt the power settle. She was stronger. She was whole.
She and Ethan kissed.
As they pulled back, she felt a strange flutter in her core—something new.
She placed a hand on her stomach.
Ethan noticed. “What is it?”
“I don’t know. A feeling.”
She looked out at the crowd. At the future.
“We’ll have time to find out,” she said.
