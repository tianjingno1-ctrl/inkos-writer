# 第10章 The Vault

The throne room was empty. The guards had been dismissed for a ceasefire meeting. Ella and Ethan lifted the stone slab hidden beneath the carpet.
A spiral staircase descended into darkness.
They climbed down, torches lighting the way. After two hundred steps, they reached a circular chamber.
At its center, a pedestal of obsidian held a sphere of liquid light.
Ancient words were carved on the wall: *For the heir who completes the prophecy, the blood of the ancestor shall reveal the peace eternal.*
Ella touched the sphere.
The room dissolved.
She saw the First King founding the Lycan nation. She saw the prophecy written in silver: *A hybrid shall be born of pure and seer. When she marks with the enemy line, the blood feud ends. The dawn wolf shall unite the packs.*
The enemy line. Ethan’s family.
She turned. “The prophecy says our families were enemies. They fought for centuries. The only way to unite the packs was for a hybrid from my line to bond with someone from yours.”
Ethan’s face went pale. “So the bond—it was prophecy.”
“It was destiny.”
The sphere cracked. A dagger emerged, its blade clear as glass. The Unity Blade, it was called. It could bind any pack to the queen by free consent.
Ella sheathed it.
They returned upstairs.
But as they stepped into the throne room, they found chaos.
Guards running. Jason met them at the door, blood on his cheek. “The Elder Council’s loyalists launched a surprise attack. They’re trying to take the palace back.”
Ethan snarled. “How many?”
“Five hundred. With a siege engine.”
Ella looked at the blade. “Then we use this.”
She rallied the hybrid and northern wolves. They formed a shield wall at the palace gates.
The loyalists charged.
Ella stood at the front. “I am the queen marked by prophecy. I carry the Unity Blade. If you fight me, you fight the will of the moon.”
The loyalists hesitated.
Then their commander screamed, “She’s a half-breed! Attack!”
They broke rank and charged.
The battle was fierce. Ella cut down five with the blade—it shimmered, and each one she struck fell paralyzed, not dead.
More wolves joined her. The tide turned.
The loyalist commander fell to Ethan’s claws. The rest surrendered.
In the silence after, Ella stood covered in blood, but none of it was hers.
She held the blade high. “This is the weapon that binds. Tomorrow, I will rewrite the law. No more blood-based privileges. Hybrid and pureblood stand equal.”
The cheer that went up shook the walls.
