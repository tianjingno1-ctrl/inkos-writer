# 第12章 The Western Pack

The western packs were a loose alliance of three clans, led by a council of alphas. They had refused Ella’s invitation to the peace summit.
Ella traveled with a small retinue: Ethan, Jason, and Bjorn. The journey took two days.
The meeting was tense. The alphas were old, pureblood, steeped in tradition.
“You may have united the north,” said their leader, a grizzled wolf named Orin. “But we do not bow to prophecy and half-blood queens.”
Ella placed the Unity Blade on the table. “I’m not asking you to bow. I’m asking you to join. If you don’t, you’ll be isolated—cut off from trade, treaties, and protection.”
Orin’s eyes narrowed. “Are you threatening us?”
“I’m stating reality.”
One of the younger alphas stood. “I say we challenge her. Prove if her prophecy is real.”
Orin considered. “One fight. If you win, we discuss terms. If you lose, you leave our territory forever.”
Ella agreed.
She faced the young alpha, a man named Rune. He shifted into a black wolf with a white chest.
She didn’t shift. She drew the Unity Blade. “I’ll use this. It won’t kill, but it will bind.”
Rune laughed—then charged.
Ella sidestepped his first lunge, slashed the blade across his leg. He stumbled. She disarmed him with a twist of the blade.
He hit the ground, paralyzed.
She stood over him. “Yield.”
He growled, but Orin raised a hand. “Enough.”
Orin looked at the blade. “I didn’t believe the relics still existed. You have the unity weapon. That proves something.” He exhaled. “We will come to the summit. But we will not swear allegiance. Not yet.”
“That’s enough for now.”
Ella sheathed the blade.
They returned home with a tentative alliance.
But news came as they arrived: the underground extremists were planning a final strike on the capital. They had captured a group of hybrid children to use as hostages.
Ella’s blood went cold.
“We have to save them.”
“We will,” Ethan said. “But we need a plan.”
